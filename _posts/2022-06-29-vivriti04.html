About the role:

The risk team is responsible for overall risk framework and strategy for the organization. The team develops various risk models, arrives at various sector reports from a risk perspective and provides in depth analysis and recommendation around risk profiles of various clients.

As the organization is embarking on the Retail journey, with greater geographical footprint of the clients, and lower average ticket size, the number of clients shall increase exponentially.

The Risk team shall have the role cut out in terms of robust diligence, analysis, faster TAT and ensuring nil delinquencies. Track record in

  • Credit appraisal or approval of Supply Chain Finance cases (Dealer/ Vendor) and SMEs as well as excellent knowledge of target industries channel’s behaviour
  • Experience in SCF Collections including process/ policy/ framework and implementation

Key Responsibilities:

  • Assessment & mitigation of risk in Anchor led business by thoroughly analysing the financials, business model, banking, hygiene checks on dealers and vendors.
  • Monitoring of SCF book and Collections: Setting up of framework for monitoring and collections and implementing the same.
  • Meeting the dealers and vendors, inspection of units, factories and go-downs for underwriting and assessment of their needs.
  • Ensure credit process and credit policy is implemented while approving and recommending supply chain cases.
  • Engaging with Business team for Anchor level assessment, program features.
  • Maintain TAT while Responsible for underwriting supply chain finance business.
  • Taking reference checks from market for dealers and suppliers.
  • Monitoring escrow churns/credits.
  • Tech build out/ credit filter coding.
  • Periodical/Annual review of existing cases.
  • Portfolio Hygiene, manage periodic Stock Audits, follow up for PDDs, insurance and escrow arrangements.
  • Providing inputs on building internal Controls and credit risk and underwriting programs for Supply chain finance
  • Tracking next due payments through MIS.
  • Eye for detail and accuracy
